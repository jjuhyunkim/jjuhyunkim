## Basic Information

> Mastrorosa, F. Kumara, et al. "Complete chromosome 21 centromere sequencing of families with Down syndrome." The American Journal of Human Genetics (2026).

---

# 1. One-Sentence Summary

> 이 연구는 trisomy chr21 (T21) 이 있는 child와 그들의 familly member들의 genome assembly를 통해 maternal meiosis I error (MMIE)에 의해 maternally inherited 되는 chr21 haplotype들을 sequence level, HOR array composition, CDR location 등을 characterize 하고 normal populuation의 chr21 centromere와 비교함.

---

# 2. Background

## Biological Problem

* 연구 대상: 8 families with a child with triosmy 21 (T21).
* 기존 지식: centromere 길이에 따라 trasmission 되는 확률이 다르다. 클수록 더 효율적으로 transmission 됨. T21이 있는 individual은 shorter chr21 centromere 를 가지는 경향이 있다.
* 아직 해결되지 않은 문제: mouse 실험에서는 이를 보인적이 있으나, complete sequence level 로는 본적이 없음. centromere 시퀀스가 high repeat 이라서 assemble 하기 어려웠기 때문. 또한 이전까지는 human diploid assembly 는 있었지만, tiromy 는 없었음.


# 3. Research Question

### Main Question

* 어떤 cetnromere variation (centromere size, sequence, alleleic differences, and epigenetic features or etc) 이 nondisjunction에 영향을 미치는가?

---

# 4. Methods

## Experimental Design

### Samples

* Organism: 8 families with a child with trisomy 21 (T21).
* Controls: normal centromere sequences (n = 287) from HPRC, HGSVC and Arab pangenome.

### Experimental Methods

* Pacbio HiFi + UL-ONT (gDNAs were extracted from the LCLs)

### Key Procedures

1. Genome assembly using Verkko
2. Repeat annotation (HOR arrays) using seuqnece and CDRs using methylation patterns
3. Normal popultion과 비교분석.

---

## Bioinformatics / Computational Analysis
### Pipeline

```text
HiFi (~59x) and ONT (>100 kb ~23x)
 ↓
Genome assembly (Verkko or hifiasm)
 ↓
contig fixing and patching (NucFreq and patching from Verkko to hifiasm assembly, vice versa)
 ↓ 
CDR annotation (CDR-Finder) / Centromere characterization (Aligning sequences against the T2T-CHM13v2.0 reference/ RepeatMasker/HumAS-HMMER)
```

---

# 5. Results

## Figure-by-Figure Summary

### Figure 1
![Figure1](https://www.cell.com/cms/10.1016/j.ajhg.2026.05.010/asset/a7c6f4e8-6d1c-4562-9533-ebe0d92c8f0a/main.assets/gr1_lrg.jpg)
**Question**

* chr21 centromeres의 inheritance 패턴이 어떤가?

**Method**
* proband의 genome을 assemble하여 compolete sequence를 사용해 methylation pattern, sequence identity, repeat composition을 확인.

**Key Result**
1. AG1677 은 maternal 에서 두개 (H1 and H2) 그리고 paternal 에서 한개 (H3) 이 inherited 됨.
2. Maternal에서 inherited 된 두개의 chr21의 centromere는 크기와 HOR array size가 꽤나 차이가 남 (H1 : 1.524 M bp vs H2 : 100 k bp, H3 : 1.471 M bp)
3. Methylation pattern으로 CDR을 찾을 수 있었는데, H1이 fragmented 되어 있는 CDR를 갖고 있었음 (H1 : 10 kbp ~ 50 kbp, H2 : 120 kbp, H3 : 135 kbp).

---

### Figure 2
![Figure2](https://www.cell.com/cms/10.1016/j.ajhg.2026.05.010/asset/47635e5c-1929-436b-970d-8c6a352cd7bb/main.assets/gr2_lrg.jpg)
**Question**

* chr21 centromeres의 inheritance 패턴이 어떤가?

**Method**

* 부모의 assembly 와 proband의 assembly를 직접적으로 비교. 

**Key Result**

1. 거의 sequence 상 차이가 없을 정도로 inheritance가 잘 되어서 parent-or-origin을 찾기 쉬움. 
2. inheritance 되지 않은 paternal H4 는 가장 작은 maternal H2의 alpha Sat 보다는 긴것을 가지고 있다.
3. CDR이 fragmented 되어 있는 maternal H1 의 패턴은 de-novo 이다.
4. complete 하게 assemble 된 다른 chromosome의 centromere들은 이런 asymmetric pattern을 보이지 않음.

### Figure 3
![Figure3](https://www.cell.com/cms/10.1016/j.ajhg.2026.05.010/asset/168903a5-4f8d-4ad0-8d2d-f0ed00ecc067/main.assets/gr3_lrg.jpg)
**Question**

* 다른 Down syndrome familly 에서는 chr21 centromere 가 어떻게 생겼나.

**Method**

* 다른 Down syndrome sample 들도 마찬가지로 genome assembly 진행해서 직접 비교

**Key Result**

AG16777과 유사한 결과가 replicated 됨.
* 3개의 chr21 centromere 중에서 두 개는 maternally inherited, 한개는 paternally inherited. 2개의 maternal centromere는 HOR array 크기가 크게 차이남 (181 kbp vs 3.5 Mbp).
* 3개의 haplotype 중에서 maternally inherited 된 chr21 centromere 한개는 proband에서만 specific 하게 fragmented CDR를 가지고 있음.
* complete 하게 assemble 된 다른 chromosome의 centromere들은 이런 asymmetric pattern을 보이지 않음.

### Figure 4
**Question**

* To understand the genetic and epigenetic diverisity of chr21 centromeres in the general population

**Method**

* 1kgp sample 중에서 35 fully resolved chr21 centromere 를 선택해서 tree 만들고 a-Sat composition이랑 length를 비교

**Key Result**

* 11-mer 가 가장 많이 차지를 함. 그리고 african descent 들은 4-mer 를 더 가지고 있기도 함.
* CDR은 보통 p-arm 쪽에 위치하며 (91%), 1~4 개, 90~335 kbp 정도 span한다. CDR이 위치하는 canonical HOR array 는 11-mer이지만 몇개의 haplotype에서는 4-mer랑 5-mer에서도 CDR이 보이긴 함.
* q-arm의 20 kbp flanking pericentromeric seuqnece를 가지고 phylogenic tree 그렸더니, triomsy chr21들이 최근에 evole된 clade에 enriched 되어 있음 (17kya).

### Figure 5

**Question**

* human population 에서 baseline centromere length distribution을 구축하기 위해.

**Method**

* T21 centromere를 287 completely assembled chr21 centromere와 비교. (from CHM13, Arab pangenome, HPRC, HGSVC)

**Key Result**

* length 자체는 유의하게 다르지 않음 (p=0.9). 마찬가지로 작은 centromere (< 500 bbp)의 빈도수 또한 T21 cohort vs population 에서 유의하게 다르지 않음.
* 하지만, chr21 centromere size asymmetry는 유의하게 차이가 있음. ~~T21에서 두 샘플만 큰 차이가 있는것 아닌가? 샘플 수가 부족하다는 것은 논문에서도 limitation으로 언급됨~~

# 6. Major Findings

## Finding 1

이전 연구에서 밝혀졌던 것 처럼, chr21 centromere는 length, a-Sat HOR array structure 그리고 CDR pattern에서 variable함.

> Chromosomes 3, 4 and 21 represent some of the smallest α-satellite HOR arrays and show the greatest variation in length among human haplotypes  (Logsdon, Glennis A., et al. "The variation and evolution of complete human centromeres." Nature 629.8010 (2024): 136-145.)

---

## Finding 2

이전 T21 연구에서는 shorter centromere가 T21 generation에 영향을 주는 것이라고 가설이 세워졌지만, 이 연구에서는 오히려 shorter centromere의 빈도수는 normal cohort와 크게 다르지 않지만, 2개의 maternal haplotype 간의 a-Sat length의 asymmetry가 T21에서 크다는 것을 발견함 (2 of 7).

한 가지 제안하는 가설은, chr21 centromere의 a-Sat의 길이가 차이가 많이나면, homologous alignment와 pairing의 효율성을 낮춰서 meiotic 동안 synapsis formation을 방해할 수 있다. 이것이 maternal meiotic error 을 만들어서 2개의 chr21이 child한테 inherited 하게 하는 영향을 줄 수 있다.

하지만 이런 비대칭성은 7명 중에 단 2 명의 mother에서만 발견이 되었기 때문에 generalize 하기는 어렵다.

---

## Basic Information

> Mastrorosa, F. Kumara, et al. "Complete chromosome 21 centromere sequencing of families with Down syndrome." The American Journal of Human Genetics (2026).

---

# 1. One-Sentence Summary

> This study generated genome assemblies of children with trisomy 21 (T21) and their family members, then characterized maternally inherited chr21 haplotypes at sequence resolution (including HOR-array composition and CDR location) to compare them against normal-population chr21 centromeres.

---

# 2. Background

## Biological Problem

* Study cohort: 8 families with a child affected by trisomy 21 (T21).
* Existing knowledge: centromere length can influence transmission efficiency; larger centromeres may transmit more efficiently. Individuals with T21 were previously suggested to have shorter chr21 centromeres.
* Remaining gap: this pattern had not been examined comprehensively at complete sequence level in humans, mainly because centromeres are highly repetitive and difficult to assemble. Also, full trisomy-resolved assemblies were not previously available.


# 3. Research Question

### Main Question

* Which centromere features (size, sequence, allelic differences, epigenetic features, etc.) influence nondisjunction?

---

# 4. Methods

## Experimental Design

### Samples

* Organism: 8 families with a child with trisomy 21 (T21).
* Controls: 287 normal centromere sequences from HPRC, HGSVC, and the Arab pangenome.

### Experimental Methods

* PacBio HiFi + UL-ONT (gDNA extracted from LCLs)

### Key Procedures

1. Genome assembly with Verkko.
2. Repeat annotation of HOR arrays by sequence and CDR annotation using methylation patterns.
3. Comparative analysis against normal-population centromeres.

---

## Bioinformatics / Computational Analysis

### Pipeline

```text
HiFi (~59x) and ONT (>100 kb ~23x)
 ↓
Genome assembly (Verkko or hifiasm)
 ↓
Contig fixing and patching (NucFreq; cross-patching between Verkko and hifiasm)
 ↓
CDR annotation (CDR-Finder) / Centromere characterization (alignment to T2T-CHM13v2.0, RepeatMasker, HumAS-HMMER)
```

---

# 5. Results

## Figure-by-Figure Summary

### Figure 1
![Figure1](https://www.cell.com/cms/10.1016/j.ajhg.2026.05.010/asset/a7c6f4e8-6d1c-4562-9533-ebe0d92c8f0a/main.assets/gr1_lrg.jpg)
**Question**

* What is the inheritance pattern of chr21 centromeres?

**Method**
* Assemble the proband genome and examine methylation patterns, sequence identity, and repeat composition with complete centromere sequences.

**Key Result**
1. In AG1677, two chr21 haplotypes were maternally inherited (H1, H2), and one was paternally inherited (H3).
2. The two maternal centromeres differed greatly in size and HOR-array length (H1: 1.524 Mbp vs H2: 100 kbp; H3: 1.471 Mbp).
3. CDR annotation from methylation patterns showed a fragmented CDR in H1 (H1: 10-50 kbp, H2: 120 kbp, H3: 135 kbp).

---

### Figure 2
![Figure2](https://www.cell.com/cms/10.1016/j.ajhg.2026.05.010/asset/47635e5c-1929-436b-970d-8c6a352cd7bb/main.assets/gr2_lrg.jpg)
**Question**

* How do inherited vs non-inherited chr21 centromeres compare?

**Method**

* Direct comparison of parental assemblies and proband assemblies.

**Key Result**

1. Sequence-level inheritance was highly faithful, making parent-of-origin assignment straightforward.
2. The non-inherited paternal H4 still had a longer alpha-satellite array than the smallest maternal H2.
3. The fragmented CDR pattern in maternal H1 appears to be de novo.
4. Other fully assembled chromosome centromeres did not show this strong asymmetry.

---

### Figure 3
![Figure3](https://www.cell.com/cms/10.1016/j.ajhg.2026.05.010/asset/168903a5-4f8d-4ad0-8d2d-f0ed00ecc067/main.assets/gr3_lrg.jpg)
**Question**

* What do chr21 centromeres look like in other Down syndrome families?

**Method**

* Assemble additional Down syndrome samples and compare centromeres directly.

**Key Result**

The AG1677 pattern was replicated.
* Among three chr21 centromeres, two were maternally inherited and one paternally inherited; maternal centromeres showed strong HOR-size asymmetry (181 kbp vs 3.5 Mbp).
* One maternally inherited centromere showed a proband-specific fragmented CDR.
* Other completely assembled chromosome centromeres did not show this asymmetric pattern.

---

### Figure 4
**Question**

* To understand genetic and epigenetic diversity of chr21 centromeres in the general population.

**Method**

* Built a tree from 35 fully resolved chr21 centromeres in 1KGP and compared alpha-satellite composition and length.

**Key Result**

* 11-mer HOR units were most common; some African-descent haplotypes were enriched for 4-mer patterns.
* CDRs were usually on the p-arm side (91%), with 1-4 CDRs spanning ~90-335 kbp. Canonical CDR-associated HOR was mostly 11-mer, with occasional 4-mer/5-mer CDRs.
* A phylogenetic tree from q-arm 20 kbp pericentromeric flanks suggested trisomy chr21s are enriched in a recently evolved clade (~17 kya).

---

### Figure 5

**Question**

* To establish baseline centromere length distribution in humans.

**Method**

* Compared T21 centromeres against 287 completely assembled chr21 centromeres (CHM13, Arab pangenome, HPRC, HGSVC).

**Key Result**

* Centromere length itself was not significantly different (p = 0.9), and the frequency of small centromeres (<500 kbp) was also not significantly different between T21 and the population cohort.
* However, chr21 centromere size asymmetry was significantly higher in T21 samples.

---

# 6. Major Findings

## Finding 1

As shown in prior work, chr21 centromeres are highly variable in length, alpha-satellite HOR structure, and CDR pattern.

> Chromosomes 3, 4 and 21 represent some of the smallest alpha-satellite HOR arrays and show the greatest variation in length among human haplotypes (Logsdon et al., Nature 2024).

---

## Finding 2

Earlier T21 studies proposed that shorter centromeres may contribute to T21 generation. In this study, the frequency of short centromeres was not different from controls, but asymmetry in alpha-satellite length between the two maternal haplotypes was larger in T21 (2 of 7).

One hypothesis is that large alpha-satellite length asymmetry between homologs may reduce homologous alignment/pairing efficiency and interfere with meiotic synapsis, increasing maternal meiotic error and resulting in dual chr21 inheritance by the child.

However, this asymmetry was observed in only 2 of 7 mothers, so generalization is still limited.

---
